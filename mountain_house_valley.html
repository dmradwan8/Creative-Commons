Sharing Creative Works with the World

Creative Commons gives everyone from individual creators to large companies and institutions a simple, standardized way to grant copyright permissions to their creative work. Creative Commons licenses helps creators share their works and create new works based on the works of others.

To choose a license, answer these questions:

  • Do I want to allow people to copy, distribute, display, or perform my work (“use”)?
  • Do I want to allow modifications to be made of my work (“remix”)?
  • What kind of license do I want? A license that is all-in-one, or a suite of separate licenses?
  • Do I want to impose any conditions on people who use my work?

Types of Creative Commons Licenses

Attribution (by)

This is the license that allows others to use work as long as they give the original author credit.

Attribution Share Alike (by-sa)

This license gives others the right to use work as long as they give the original author credit and their new work is shared under the same license.

Attribution Non-commercial (by-nc)

This license allows others to use work as long as they give the original author credit and their use is for non-commercial purposes.

Attribution Non-commercial Share Alike (by-nc-sa)

This license allows others to use work as long as they give the original author credit, their use is for non-commercial purposes, and their new work is shared under the same license.

Attribution Non-commercial No Derivatives (by-nc-nd)

This license allows others to use work as long as they give the original author credit and their use is for non-commercial purposes, but they don’t have the right to modify or change the work in any way.

Creative Commons 4.0 Licenses

CC BY

This license lets others distribute, remix, tweak, and build upon the creator’s work, even commercially, as long as they credit the creator for the original creation.

CC BY-SA

This license lets others remix, tweak, and build upon the creator’s work even for commercial reasons, as long as they credit the creator and license their new creation under the identical terms.

CC BY-ND

This license allows for redistribution, commercial and non-commercial, as long as it is passed along unchanged and in whole, with credit to the creator.

CC BY-NC

This license lets others remix, tweak, and build upon the creator’s work noncommercially, and although their new works must also acknowledge the creator and be noncommercial, they don’t have to license their derivative works on the same terms.

CC BY-NC-SA

This license lets others remix, tweak, and build upon the creator’s work noncommercially, as long as they credit the creator and license their new creation under the identical terms.

CC BY-NC-ND

This license is the most restrictive of the 6 main CC licenses. It only allows others to download the works and share them with others as long as they credit the creator, but they can’t change them in any way or use them commercially.
